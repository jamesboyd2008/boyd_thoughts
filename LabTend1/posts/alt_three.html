Third Post

   Conciously understanding the process by which people make decisions is an interest of mine. I'm not confident that I understand the very nature of being human, let alone the inner workings of people's minds. I can function in society. I can hold a job. I understand people's motives enough to get by, but a deeper understanding beckons me.

   Its my perception that the deeper one delves into a particular train of thought, the higher the risk they run of flying wildly off course from that which is truthful. Here's an example:



   I enjoy living under the pretense that causality is universal. I draw out this notion so far as to dare to believe that, even in the 21st century, events happen for reasons. I relish in the joy, be it foolish or otherwise, that humans/humanity has a purpose. Please don't hold your breath in hopes of finding that purpose, or conviction of it's existence, within this blog.

   Ultimately, I believe understanding itself is some kind of luxury. One needn't understand particle physics to breathe. One needn't understand a person to love them. One needn't understand logic to blog.
